10 Uncommon Filipino Words

November 26, 2021 posted by Anjaneth S. Nazareno

The words that are unusually Filipino in the Philippines are those that have already been forgotten or changed as a result of society.As we all know, when an economy keeps on growing, their language will also change to get out of poverty, and because of these changes, some of the words are now dead or changed. It is also because of the technology that is already expanding all over the world that we should know some of these uncommon Filipino words. In order for you to have knowledge of these words, here are the words you can't imagine that you use on a daily basis.

1.) Payneta

English translation: Comb

Description: A strip of plastic, metal, or wood with a row of narrow teeth, used for untangling or arranging the hair (Source: Oxford Languages)

Sentence: Ang payneta na ito ay isang metal at kulay ginto.

2.) Panginain

English translation: Browser

Description: A computer program that is used to find and look at information on the Internet. (Source: Merriam-Webster)

Sentence: Magsaliksik ng mga impormasyon, maaring gumamit ng libro o panginain.

3.) Alimusom

English translation: Scent

Description: A distinctive smell especially one that is pleasant. (Source: Oxford Languages)

Sentence: Ang alimusom nitong pabango ay nakakaadik, tila parang bulaklak na bagong pitas.

4.) Sulatroniko

English translation: E-mail

Description: A system for sending messages from one computer to another computer. (Source: Merriam-Webster)

Sentence: Ang grupo nila Galang ay gumamit ng sulatroniko upang manghingi ng maaring pagkuhanan ng kanila gagamitin sa kanilang pananaliksik.

5.) Sambat

English translation: Fork

Description: An implement with two or more prongs used for lifting food to the mouth or holding it when cutting. (Source: Oxford Languages)

Sentence: Pinasuyo kay Lanz na kumuha ng sambat para sa kanilang bisita.

6.) Miktinig

English translation: Microphone

Description: An instrument whereby sound waves are caused to generate or modulate an electric current usually for the purpose of transmitting or recording sound (as speech or music). (Source: Merriam-Webster)

Sentence: Mas malakas pa ang boses nito kaysa sa miktinig.

7.) Duyog

English translation: Eclipse

Description: An occasion when the sun looks like it is completely or partially covered with a dark circle because the moon is between the sun and the Earth. (Source: Merriam-Webster)

Sentence: Lumabas si Ricamae upang kanyang masaksihan ang kagandahan ng duyog sa kalangitan.

8.) Himaton

English translation: Clue

Description: A piece of evidence or information used in the detection of a crime or solving of a mystery. (Source: Oxford Languages)

Sentence: Hindi mahanap ang matatag na himaton ng mga kapulisan sa isang malubhang krimen.

9.) Di-mataga

English Translation: Handsome young man

Description: A well-dressed, good-looking young man

Sentence: Ang binatang si Aaron ay di-mataga ngunit masungit.

10.) Antipara

English translation: Eyeglasses

Description: A pair of lenses set in a frame resting on the nose and ears, used to correct or assist defective eyesight or protect the eyes. (Source: Oxford Languages)

Sentence: Muling na hulog ni Same-well ang kanyang antipara kaya't sya ay napagalitang ng kanyang nakatatandang kapatid.

In present society, it doesn't really matter anymore if we still use these words, as far as communication and expression are concerned.
